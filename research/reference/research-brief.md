## Section 2: External Cause → Google Ads Effect Chain

BrightMatter must distinguish environmental changes from account-level issues. For each cause below, research: how to detect it, what it looks like inside Google Ads, and what the correct response is (vs. the common misdiagnosis).

---

### 2.1 Tracking & Measurement Breaks

**External cause:** Website update breaks GTM tag, conversion tracking code removed, thank-you page URL changed, form provider switched, payment gateway change breaks purchase tracking, Consent Mode misconfiguration after privacy update.

**Google Ads effect:** Conversions drop to zero or near-zero across all campaigns simultaneously. CPA appears to spike infinitely. Smart Bidding enters learning mode or stops spending because it can't see conversions.

**How BrightMatter should detect:** Conversions dropping >80% across ALL campaigns in the same account on the same day = tracking break, not performance decline. Cross-reference with click volume (if clicks are stable but conversions dropped, it's measurement). If available, compare GA4 conversion data to Google Ads conversion data — divergence indicates tracking issue.

**Common misdiagnosis:** "Our campaigns stopped working" → team pauses campaigns, changes bids, rewrites ads — all wasted effort when the real fix is a tag repair.

**Research task:** Document the top 10 tracking break patterns and their signatures in Google Ads data. How quickly does Smart Bidding react to a tracking break? What's the recovery time after a fix?

---

### 2.2 Website & Landing Page Changes

**External cause:** Site redesign, CMS migration, new landing page template, page speed degradation, mobile experience change, checkout flow modification, form field additions, pop-up/interstitial additions, hosting change, CDN change.

**Google Ads effect:** Conversion rate drops (same traffic, fewer conversions). Quality Score landing page component degrades. CPA rises. Smart Bidding may increase bids to compensate (spending more to hit targets on a worse-converting page).

**How BrightMatter should detect:** Conversion rate drop isolated to specific landing page URLs while traffic volume and CTR remain stable = landing page issue. Core Web Vitals degradation correlating with conversion rate decline. If GA4 available: bounce rate spike on specific pages.

**Common misdiagnosis:** "CPA is rising, we need to lower bids" → lowering bids reduces traffic to a page that needs fixing, making the problem worse.

**Research task:** Quantify the relationship between page speed changes and conversion rate impact. What Core Web Vitals thresholds trigger meaningful performance degradation? How long does Quality Score take to update after a landing page change?

---

### 2.3 Industry-Wide CPC Inflation

**External cause:** Macroeconomic inflation raising cost of all ad inventory. New competitors entering the market across a vertical. Seasonal demand peaks (Q4, tax season, back-to-school). Google SERP layout changes reducing available ad slots.

**Google Ads effect:** CPC rises across all keywords. Impression share may drop if budgets stay flat. CPA rises even though conversion rates are unchanged. Looks like the account is getting worse when actually the market got more expensive.

**How BrightMatter should detect:** CPC increase affecting ALL accounts in the same vertical simultaneously = macro, not account. Compare account CPC trends to Auction Insights — if all competitors are also paying more, it's market-level. Cross-reference with Tinuiti/Merkle quarterly benchmark data.

**Common misdiagnosis:** "Our account is losing efficiency" → team over-optimizes individual keywords when the correct response is budget reallocation or target adjustment.

**Research task:** Build a CPC trend baseline by vertical using benchmark reports. What's the normal CPC inflation rate per vertical per year? When does a CPC increase exceed the macro baseline and indicate an account-specific issue?

---

### 2.4 Competitor Entry, Exit, or Strategy Change

**External cause:** New competitor starts bidding on your keywords. Existing competitor dramatically increases budget. Competitor launches aggressive promotion. Competitor exits the auction (acquisition, shutdown, budget cut).

**Google Ads effect:** Impression share changes. CPC shifts on specific keyword clusters. Position shifts in Auction Insights. Competitor exit = sudden CPC decrease and impression share increase (opportunity). Competitor entry = opposite.

**How BrightMatter should detect:** Auction Insights overlap rate and outranking share changes for specific competitors correlated with CPC or impression share changes. A new entity appearing in Auction Insights with >10% overlap = new competitor entry.

**Common misdiagnosis:** "Our CPC spiked on these keywords" → team adds negative keywords or reduces bids when the correct response might be to adjust targets to maintain position or shift to less contested terms.

**Research task:** What Auction Insights patterns reliably indicate competitor strategic changes? How should bid strategy respond to different competitive scenarios?

---

### 2.5 Google Platform & Algorithm Changes

**External cause:** Smart Bidding model updates. Match type behavior changes (broad match expansion). SERP layout changes (AI Overviews, fewer/more ad slots). New campaign types or features (AI Max, Demand Gen updates). Policy enforcement changes. Auto-applied recommendations.

**Google Ads effect:** Performance shifts that appear across multiple accounts with no account-level changes. CTR changes when SERP layout changes. Match type suddenly matching different queries. Features enabled without manual intervention (auto-applied recommendations).

**How BrightMatter should detect:** Performance shift across multiple accounts in the same timeframe with no logged changes in any account = platform change. Cross-reference with Google Ads announcements timeline. Check change history for auto-applied recommendations that weren't manually approved.

**Common misdiagnosis:** "Something broke in our account" → team spends hours diagnosing when the answer is a platform-wide change affecting everyone.

**Research task:** Build a Google Ads platform change timeline (2024-2026) with dates and descriptions. For each major change, document the typical performance impact pattern. Which changes affected which campaign types?

---

### 2.6 Seasonality & Calendar Events

**External cause:** Predictable annual demand cycles (Q4 holiday, Q1 post-holiday dip, summer slowdown, back-to-school, tax season). Weather events (HVAC, seasonal products). Cultural events and holidays by geography. Day-of-week and time-of-day patterns.

**Google Ads effect:** Traffic volume and conversion rate fluctuations that follow calendar patterns. Q1 looks terrible compared to Q4 but is actually normal. Weekend performance differs from weekday performance.

**How BrightMatter should detect:** Year-over-year comparison for the same calendar period. Vertical-specific seasonal baselines (ecommerce peaks Q4, B2B dips in summer, tax services peak Feb-April). Compare current performance to the same week last year, not last month.

**Common misdiagnosis:** "Performance is declining in January" → team makes aggressive changes to campaigns that are behaving normally for the season.

**Research task:** Build seasonal baseline curves by vertical using historical data across the 500 accounts. What are the standard seasonal patterns for each major vertical? What magnitude of seasonal swing is normal?

---

### 2.7 Privacy & Tracking Infrastructure Changes

**External cause:** iOS App Tracking Transparency (ATT) opt-outs. Consent Mode v2 implementation. Third-party cookie deprecation. Browser tracking prevention updates. Ad blocker adoption increases.

**Google Ads effect:** Reported conversions decline while actual business outcomes may be stable. Smart Bidding receives fewer conversion signals, potentially degrading optimization. Modeled conversions fill some gaps but introduce uncertainty.

**Common misdiagnosis:** "Our conversion rate dropped 20%" → may actually reflect 20% of conversions becoming invisible to tracking, not an actual performance decline.

**How BrightMatter should detect:** Conversion decline not matched by corresponding decline in actual revenue/leads (CRM data). Growing gap between Google Ads reported conversions and CRM-confirmed outcomes. Consent rate changes correlating with conversion tracking changes.

**Research task:** Quantify the typical measurement gap by industry and traffic source. What percentage of actual conversions are typically invisible to Google Ads tracking in 2026? How does this vary by device and geography?

---

### 2.8 Search Demand Shifts

**External cause:** Google Trends volume changes for key terms. Viral content (positive or negative) about your brand or product category. News events affecting your industry. Cultural shifts in how people search (voice search, AI chat search reducing traditional search volume).

**Google Ads effect:** Impression volume changes with no campaign setting changes. CTR changes if the composition of searchers shifts (more casual browsers vs. high-intent buyers). New query types appearing in search terms reports.

**How BrightMatter should detect:** Google Trends data for core keywords showing volume decline/spike that matches impression volume changes. Search term report showing new query clusters not previously seen. Impression changes uncorrelated with budget or bid changes.

**Research task:** How should BrightMatter integrate Google Trends data into its pattern recognition? What magnitude of search volume change is meaningful vs. noise? How do AI Overviews and AI chat search affect traditional search volume?

---

### 2.9 Product, Pricing & Inventory Changes

**External cause (ecommerce):** Price increases/decreases. Stockouts on best-selling products. Competitor pricing changes. New product launches. Promotional calendar (sales start/end). Shipping cost/speed changes. Review score changes.

**Google Ads effect:** Conversion rate changes correlated with price changes. Best-performing product groups losing traffic when products go out of stock. Shopping ad competitiveness changing when competitor prices shift. ROAS spiking during promotions and dropping after.

**How BrightMatter should detect:** Product-level performance changes correlated with Merchant Center feed changes (price, availability, title updates). Conversion rate changes on specific product groups without campaign setting changes. Promotional period performance vs. non-promotional baseline.

**Research task:** What is the typical conversion rate elasticity to price changes in ecommerce by product category? How quickly does Google's algorithm adjust to stockouts and restocks? What's the carryover effect of promotional periods on post-promotion performance?

---

### 2.10 Cross-Channel Spillover Effects

**External cause:** Meta/Facebook spend changes (budget cuts reduce brand awareness, which reduces branded search volume). Email campaign sends driving search behavior. TV/radio advertising creating search demand. PR coverage or influencer mentions. Organic social content going viral.

**Google Ads effect:** Branded search volume changes without any Google Ads changes. Non-brand conversion rate changes (awareness campaigns on other channels warm up audiences, making them more likely to convert on Google). Overall ROAS shifts that can't be explained by Google Ads activity alone.

**How BrightMatter should detect:** Brand search volume correlation with Meta/social spend levels. Conversion rate changes on non-brand campaigns correlated with email send volume or social content publishing. Multi-touch attribution paths showing other channels appearing before Google Ads conversion.

**Common misdiagnosis:** "Our Google Ads ROAS is declining" when actually Meta awareness spend was cut and the downstream effect hit Google.

**Research task:** Quantify the typical cross-channel spillover effect. When Meta awareness spend is cut by X%, how much does Google branded search volume decline? What's the lag time between cross-channel changes and Google Ads impact?

---

### 2.11 Auto-Applied Recommendations & Account Defaults

**External cause:** Google auto-applies recommendations (adding keywords, changing bid targets, enabling features, expanding match types, creating new assets). Google rep makes changes during account review. Default settings on new campaign types that aren't caught during setup.

**Google Ads effect:** Performance shifts with no logged HUMAN change. Budget may suddenly shift. New keywords appear. Bid targets change. Features get enabled that weren't intended.

**How BrightMatter should detect:** Change history entries with "Auto-applied" or "Google Ads" as the actor (not a named user). Performance shifts correlated with change history timestamps where no team member made the change.

**Research task:** What are all the auto-applied recommendation types Google offers? Which ones are known to hurt performance when applied blindly? What's the recommended opt-out strategy?

---

### 2.12 Conversion Definition & Attribution Changes

**External cause:** New conversion action added to account. Primary/secondary designation changed. Conversion window modified. Attribution model switched (last-click to data-driven). GA4 imported goals added or removed. Conversion value rules changed.

**Google Ads effect:** Reported conversion count changes without any actual change in campaign performance. Smart Bidding starts optimizing for a different signal. CPA/ROAS metrics shift because the denominator (what counts as a conversion) changed.

**How BrightMatter should detect:** Conversion action change log showing additions, removals, or designation changes correlated with performance metric shifts. Sudden change in conversion volume that's exactly proportional to a new conversion action being added or removed.

**Research task:** How quickly does Smart Bidding react to conversion definition changes? What's the recommended process for changing conversion goals without destabilizing campaigns? What are the signs that a conversion definition change is affecting bidding behavior?
