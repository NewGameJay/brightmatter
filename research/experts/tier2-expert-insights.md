## Jyll Saskin Gales

### Key Position / Contrarian Take

**"Manual bidding in Google Ads is a mistake"** and **"The keyword-less future is already here."**

Gales argues that keywords as traditionally understood are becoming obsolete. With exact match now meaning "same meaning or intent" rather than literal matches, and Google aggressively pushing Broad Match + AI Max, she frames the shift as moving from keyword control to intent-and-context targeting. Her unpopular opinion: advertisers should stop fighting this transition and instead focus their energy on the three levers they can actually control.

She also strongly advises creating campaigns "without guidance" (bypassing Google's setup wizard) to retain access to all available settings, and warns against Auto-Apply Recommendations, calling them "generic across all accounts" with potential to significantly harm performance (e.g., converting all keywords to Broad Match or changing bid strategies without context).

### Specific Data or Frameworks Published

**3 Biggest PMax Levers Framework:**
1. **Conversion settings** — what you measure determines what the algorithm optimizes for
2. **Bid strategy** — the rules governing how aggressively the system pursues those conversions
3. **Assets** — the creative inputs the system has to work with

**Bid Strategy Transition Protocol:**
- After 30 conversions in 30 days → switch from Maximize Conversions to Target CPA
- Set Target CPA at actual historical CPA, then stabilize for 5–7 days
- Adjust targets in 10–20% increments, allowing 1–2 weeks between adjustments
- Never use experiments when testing Maximize bid strategies (splits budget, reduces learning)

Quoted in WordStream 2025 Benchmarks: "Rising CPCs are one of the only constants in Google Ads, so it's refreshing to see advertisers focusing on what they can control in response: conversion rates." Recommends landing page optimization, compelling creative, and offline conversion tracking as CVR improvement tools.

### Relevance to BrightMatter

Her 3-lever framework provides a diagnostic structure for pattern detection: across 500 accounts, BrightMatter can classify accounts by which lever is the primary performance driver (or bottleneck). The bid strategy transition protocol (30 conversions → tCPA → 10–20% adjustments) is a testable pattern — do accounts that follow this sequence outperform those that don't? Her "keyword-less future" thesis means BrightMatter should weight audience signals and conversion settings more heavily than keyword granularity in its pattern recognition.

## Navah Hopkins

### Key Position / Contrarian Take

**"The era of account management through micromanagement is dead"** and **consolidation isn't always the answer for PMax.**

Hopkins challenges the industry-standard advice to consolidate PMax campaigns. Her 9,199-account Optmyzr study found that **multiple campaigns with a single asset group** delivered the best ROAS, second-highest conversion rate, and lowest CPA — contradicting Google's consolidation guidance. She also argues that PPC professionals must shift from execution (keyword sculpting, manual bid adjustments) to strategy (business profitability analysis, channel fit, user behavior understanding), with machines handling real-time bidding and pattern recognition.

She warns that message mapping must now "account for systems that assemble ads dynamically rather than follow static instructions" — a fundamental reframe of creative strategy.

### Specific Data or Frameworks Published

**Optmyzr PMax Study (9,199 accounts, 24,702 campaigns):**
- 4 structural archetypes: Starter (1 campaign/1 asset group), Focused (multiple campaigns/1 asset group), Conversion Hungry (1 campaign/multiple asset groups), Mixed
- **Focused structure won** on ROAS, near-best on CVR and CPA
- 82% of advertisers ran PMax alongside other campaign types
- 51% allocated >50% of budget to PMax, achieving strongest ROAS (652.03%) but mixed CVR/CPA
- 10–25% PMax budget allocation showed best conversion rate and CPA performance

**10 Underutilized Levers** (Search Engine Journal): competitor audience segments, search term attribute filters, negatives at every structural level.

Quoted in WordStream benchmarks noting Real Estate CPCs are surprisingly affordable, questioning whether PMax adoption is driving lower average CPCs in feed-based verticals.

### Relevance to BrightMatter

The 4-archetype PMax structural classification is directly applicable as a segmentation schema across 500 accounts. BrightMatter can test whether the "Focused" structure consistently outperforms at scale, and whether the 10–25% budget allocation sweet spot holds across verticals. Her "dynamic message assembly" insight means BrightMatter should track asset-level performance patterns, not just campaign-level metrics.

## Ameet Khabra

### Key Position / Contrarian Take

**"Your PMax ROAS is a lie."**

Khabra's core contrarian position is that Performance Max campaigns systematically inflate ROAS by cannibalizing brand traffic. PMax captures existing branded intent — people already searching for your brand name — and takes credit for conversions that would have happened anyway through dedicated brand Search campaigns. The dashboard number looks impressive (e.g., 12x ROAS) but masks the reality that PMax isn't creating new demand.

He frames brand exclusions not as an optimization tactic but as "a mark of PPC maturity" and predicts that after implementing exclusions, advertisers should expect "a short-term drop in ROAS and rise in CPA — that's not failure, it's clarity."

### Specific Data or Frameworks Published

**Brand Exclusion Framework:**
- Brand exclusions work at the brand level (not individual keywords) via a structured Brand List
- Apply when running a dedicated branded Search campaign alongside PMax
- Use to isolate non-brand traffic and measure actual prospecting performance
- Expected outcome: ROAS drops, CPA rises — but reporting becomes honest

**PMax Channel Reporting (2025 updates):**
- Channel-level metric visibility: Search, Shopping, Display, YouTube, Discovery, Gmail, Maps, Search Partners
- Full search term reporting with performance metrics and direct negative keyword management
- Negative keyword capacity increased from 100 to 10,000 per campaign with match type support
- Asset-level reporting for individual headlines, descriptions, images, videos, extensions

### Relevance to BrightMatter

The "ROAS is a lie" thesis is a critical pattern detection target. Across 500 accounts, BrightMatter can identify accounts where PMax brand cannibalization is inflating metrics by comparing brand vs. non-brand conversion attribution. The channel reporting framework provides the granularity needed to detect spend concentration patterns and identify accounts where PMax is concentrating spend in low-value placements.

## Reva Minkoff

### Key Position / Contrarian Take

**"Rigid annual budget planning is dangerous for PPC"** and PMax requires conversion tracking maturity most advertisers lack.

Minkoff argues that the pace of Google Ads platform changes has made traditional annual budget planning actively harmful. If 2026 budgets were set in mid-2025, they were finalized before AI Max, Asset Studio, and emerging platforms like ChatGPT Ads existed — meaning companies can't capitalize on new opportunities until the next planning cycle. She advocates for building "flexibility funds" into annual budgets specifically earmarked for new initiatives.

On PMax, Digital4Startups emphasizes that PMax requires 50–100 conversions per month (minimum 30) and full-funnel conversion tracking before it can perform. Their guidance warns that lead forms "often see issues with bots" requiring protections, and that budget adjustments mid-campaign can force re-entry into the learning phase (1–2 week performance disruption).

### Specific Data or Frameworks Published

**PMax Readiness Checklist:**
- Conversion tracking: 50–100 conversions/month ideal, 30 minimum
- Bidding strategy set, location settings to "presence only"
- Audience signals and extensions configured
- URL expansion turned off
- Asset requirements: up to 15 headlines (≤30 chars), 1–5 long headlines (≤90 chars), 2–5 descriptions (≤60 chars), ≤20 images, ≤5 YouTube videos (min 10 seconds)
- 2-week no-touch window after launch

**Budget Flexibility Framework:**
- Don't set detailed digital budgets >6 months in advance
- Earmark explicit "new initiative" budget for platform changes
- Treat digital budgets with the real-time flexibility that real-time auctions demand

### Relevance to BrightMatter

Minkoff's readiness checklist provides a gating function for BrightMatter's recommendations: accounts below the conversion threshold shouldn't be evaluated on the same PMax criteria as mature accounts. The 2-week learning phase pattern is testable across 500 accounts — do accounts that touch PMax campaigns within the first 14 days show measurably worse outcomes? Her budget flexibility thesis suggests BrightMatter should track budget change frequency as a performance signal.

## Amy Hebdon

### Key Position / Contrarian Take

**"Craft ads that qualify the right clicks and intentionally discourage the wrong ones"** — ads should be filters, not funnels.

Hebdon's contrarian position is that most advertisers optimize for maximizing clicks, when they should be optimizing for repelling unqualified traffic. In an era of rising CPCs, the highest-leverage move isn't getting more clicks cheaper — it's ensuring every click has conversion potential. She advocates for "qualifying ad copy" that explicitly signals price points, requirements, or audience fit to filter out low-intent searchers before they cost you money.

On automation, she takes a cautious middle ground: AI tools are useful for persona research, copywriting iteration, and client communication, but the risk of "giving up too much control" is real, especially with Google's reduced search term visibility making it harder to spot and block irrelevant queries.

### Specific Data or Frameworks Published

**Segmentation-First Account Architecture:**
- Hebdon's "Magic of Segmentation" framework emphasizes granular campaign segmentation based on audience intent, not just keyword themes
- Data visualization approach to PPC reporting that focuses on month-over-month comparisons for trend detection
- 22+ years of account management across Fortune 50 to startups informs a practical framework for adapting strategy to account size

**Cold-Start Smart Bidding Protocol (synthesis from her advocacy and industry data):**
- Start with Maximize Conversions (no target) for accounts with <15 conversions/30 days
- Use micro-conversions (phone clicks, form views, time on page) to accelerate learning
- Build aggressive negative keyword lists (500+ before launch) to ensure data quality
- Set Target CPA 10–20% higher than historical average when transitioning, giving algorithm "breathing room"
- Avoid PMax for small budgets — concentrate on Search to build conversion data first

### Relevance to BrightMatter

Hebdon's "qualifying clicks" philosophy inverts the typical optimization goal and could be detected as a pattern: accounts with intentionally lower CTR but higher CVR may be following this strategy. For BrightMatter's cold-start detection, her framework provides a diagnostic checklist — are small accounts making the right sequence of moves? The micro-conversion acceleration pattern is testable across the 500-account dataset.

## Chris Ridley

### Key Position / Contrarian Take

**"Stop paying twice — automate the paid/organic cannibalization decision"** and build systematic tools to manage the AI-era complexity.

Ridley's most distinctive contribution is his Search Cannibalization Management Tool, which automatically pauses/enables paid keywords based on organic ranking performance. His contrarian position: most advertisers are wasting money bidding on terms where they already rank #1 organically. Rather than applying blanket rules, he built an automated system (SEOMonitor + Google Sheets + Google Ads scripts) that makes the paid/organic decision dynamically per keyword based on actual ranking data.

He was named one of Marketing O'Clock's Top 50 Most Influential PPC Experts and has spoken at BrightonSEO, HeroConf, and PPC Live on Performance Max management.

### Specific Data or Frameworks Published

**Search Cannibalization Management Tool:**
- Integrates SEOMonitor, Google Sheets, and Google Ads scripts
- Automatically pauses paid keywords when organic rankings are strong
- Enables paid ads only when they provide incremental value
- Case study: delivered **113% revenue growth and 27x ROAS** for Puck Stop by eliminating paid/organic overlap waste

**AI Overviews Impact (industry data Ridley operates within):**
- Paid CTR plummeted 68% on queries triggering AI Overviews: from 19.7% (June 2024) to 6.34% (September 2025)
- July 2025: paid CTR collapsed from 11% to 3% in a single month as Google expanded AI Overviews to commercial/navigational queries
- Vertical penetration: Finance 79% on longer searches, Retail 84% on comparison queries, Healthcare high even on short queries
- PMax now accounts for 62% of all Google Ads clicks and is the de facto default

### Relevance to BrightMatter

Ridley's cannibalization tool represents a pattern BrightMatter should detect at scale: which accounts are paying for clicks they'd get organically? The 27x ROAS case study from eliminating this waste suggests significant optimization opportunity across 500 accounts. The AI Overviews CTR collapse data (68% drop) provides critical context for any BrightMatter trend analysis — accounts showing CTR declines may not have a campaign problem; they may have an AI Overview displacement problem that requires structural response.
